The visualization reveals a stark contrast between L1 (Lasso) and L2 (Ridge) regularization behaviors. In the L1 plot, we observe 'sparsity' in action: as $C$ decreases (stronger regularization), coefficients are driven to exactly zero one by one, effectively performing automated feature selection. Features like 'Total Charges' and 'Contract Type' were the last to vanish, indicating their robustness as predictors for churn. Conversely, the L2 plot demonstrates a gradual shrinkage; while coefficients diminish towards zero as $C$ drops, they never quite reach it, maintaining all features in the model but with reduced influence. For this telecom dataset, L1 regularization appears more beneficial if model parsimony and interpretability are prioritized, as it clearly isolates the most impactful drivers of customer behavior by eliminating noisy or redundant features.
